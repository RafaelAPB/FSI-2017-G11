What is Marketing?

(Hover the images with the mouse, at your right)

Concept that describes the events that occur at the interface between the organization and its customers, and its idea is based on giving customers what they want while offering a good value for money. In 1960’s E. Jerome McCarthy proposed the foundational model of marketing based on 4 P’s. They stand for:

PRODUCT

Product should answer to the customer needs.

PLACE

Product should be easily accessible to the customers.

PROMOTION

Marketing communication channels and tools should be used to promote the product or service in an appealing way to the target consumers.

PRICE

Product should present itself as having a good value for money.

Later, in 1981, Booms and Bitner expanded the model with further three P’s to deal with service Intangibility, Inseparability, Heterogeneity and Perishability


PEOPLE

Most services rely on people to perform them.

PROCESS

The process by which the service is delivered is also part of what the consumer is paying for. This was created to address Heterogeneity problems.

PHYSICAL EVIDENCE

Environment where the service occurs. This was created to address Intangibility problems.

ADVERTISEMENT

Part of the marketing process directly related to promotion is focused on creating result-oriented non-personal communication of information. Successful advertisement should be based not only on 5 M’s:

  • MISSION – Definition of the reason why we are doing marketing.
  • MONEY – Identification target markets (most profitable).
  • MESSAGE – Focus on creation persuasive messages.
  • MEDIA – Choice of the right media to deliver the message.
  • MEASURE – Identify what is working and what not.

The Marketing Legacy

What to expect from Marketing in the future?

Digital capabilities

The low use of Digital Capabilities such as digital integration in the marketing strategies, digital measurement and analytics and programmatic advertising are the current biggest bottleneck for marketing and its development.

Therefore, in the future, organizations should focus on developing this capabilities in order to provide more individualized channels and an enhanced customer experience.

Social marketing

A new way of marketing, where the goal is to influence a target audience towards a certain behaviour. The application of principles and techniques like WOM, viral and seeding, allied with marketing intelligence and smart data, provide an unique and improved customer experience.

That experience is leveraged by different distribution channels for consumers characterized by a multi-tasking behaviour.

Innovative technologies

New technologies, including location-based targeting and biometric sensors, allow for a more customized customer experience on both the multi-channel and the omni-channel, improving the way that an organization interacts with its customers.

An alternative path


Nowadays, there seems to exist a gap between marketing ads and customer attention. Also, there seems to be a lot of place to digital ads companies to fill that gap.

Google recently launched PWA (Progressive Web Apps), aimed to join the best of Apps with the best of Web.

But...



Aren't ad-blockers a serious obstacle for digital ads?

Does the marketing industry needs to be aimed necessarly to the digital zone?

How can we both overcome this challenge, while getting closer to the user?

Should we try to focus again on the physical marketing, despite its advantages?


We purpose a way of joining the best of digital ads with the best of physical ads, leveraged by an omni-channel strategy, enabled by PWA. We believe that can happen. The idea we are going to purpose is already in practise. This idea not only fights ad-blockers, by providing an alternate source of revenue, but also takes into account people's location. Bellow there is a conceptual map that can provide a high-level view of the idea.

The idea is to use the combination PWA + push-up notifications to reach Android users, according to their location.
There is how the connection happens: The potential customer is walking, looking at his/her smartphone. When the customer passes by a store which is a client of InnovativeStartup#1, he/she receives a push notification on the phone. That push notification might contain useful information (like coupons or new products) from Store#1, a client of InnovativeCompany#1.

If the potential customer is already a customer, some specific campaigns can be applied. For the user to receive the notifications, Bluetooth must be active. What happens, is that the store contains a RFID tag on the physical location. When the smartphone is in the radius of the tag, it receives a push-notification. Then, if the user comes in and uses the coupon code, the InnovativeCompany#1 may charge Store#1.

The high concept pitch for this product is Google AdWords, but for physical locations. The ClientStore#1 may have a dashboard, implemented by InnovativeCompany#1, connecting its database to the marketing application, allowing the store to deliver customized ads based on a specific algorithm or magic sauce. In our opinion, there is a lot of business opportunities and room to innovate.
